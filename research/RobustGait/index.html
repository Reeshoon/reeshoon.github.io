Abstract

Appearance-based gait recognition has shown impressive performance on controlled datasets, yet robustness under real-world corruptions remains significantly underexplored. RobustGait provides a comprehensive robustness benchmark evaluating six state-of-the-art gait recognition architectures under 15 corruption types across five severity levels. Additionally, we analyze the impact of silhouette segmentation and human parsing on downstream recognition accuracy.
